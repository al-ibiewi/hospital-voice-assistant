The Problem

Unreliable Infrastructure

Nigerian hospitals face frequent power outages and poor internet connectivity, making cloud-based assistive technology unreliable.

Understaffed Facilities

Limited nursing staff cannot respond immediately to all patient needs, especially in rural healthcare centers.

Patient Mobility Constraints

Mobility-impaired patients cannot reach call buttons during emergencies, compromising safety and dignity.

Privacy Concerns

Cloud-based voice assistants stream patient conversations to remote servers, violating medical confidentiality.

Mobile App - Multi-Modal Control

Redundancy for Critical Healthcare

Voice control fails when patients are intubated, experiencing aphasia, or in noisy environments. Our companion mobile app provides a critical backup control pathway.

Bluetooth Low Energy

Direct connection to ESP32 controller without requiring hospital WiFi. 30-meter range covers entire patient room.

State Synchronization

Real-time sync between voice commands, app controls, and physical switches. Change light via voice, see status update in app instantly.

Family Remote Access

Optional feature allowing family members to monitor patient's room status and receive emergency notifications remotely.

Accessibility First

Large touch targets, high contrast UI, and screen reader support ensure usability for elderly patients with vision impairments.

Technical Implementation

Communication Protocol

Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) for power-efficient wireless communication. Custom GATT profile for command transmission with acknowledgment.

Platform

Cross-platform development using Flutter or React Native, ensuring deployment on both Android and iOS with single codebase.

State Management

Real-time synchronization between hardware state and app UI. WebSocket fallback for WiFi-enabled environments.

Security

Encrypted BLE pairing prevents unauthorized access. Device-specific PIN codes ensure only authorized users control patient environment.
